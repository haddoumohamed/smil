Prior to the outbreak of World War II, two major military alliances had emerged that would come to dominate the global conflict. On one side was the Allied powers, which included Britain, France, the Soviet Union, and eventually the United States, China, and various other nations. The Allies were united in their opposition to the aggression of the Axis powers and their goal of defeating Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, and Imperial Japan.
On the other side was the Axis alliance, led by Germany, Italy, and Japan. These three totalitarian regimes had territorial ambitions and sought to reshape the global order through military force. Germany and Italy had been aggressively expanding their spheres of influence in Europe, while Japan was engaged in a brutal conquest of territories in Asia and the Pacific.
