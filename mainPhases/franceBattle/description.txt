In May 1940, Germany launched a massive offensive through the Low Countries, unleashing its devastating blitzkrieg tactics against the Allied forces. The German army, supported by waves of planes and tanks, swiftly overwhelmed the Dutch and Belgian defenses. As the Nazis pushed deeper into France, the French army and British Expeditionary Force were forced into a desperate retreat. Trapped at Dunkirk, over 300,000 Allied troops were evacuated in a miraculous operation, but the French army was left shattered. Within just six weeks, the French government had surrendered, leaving Britain as the lone Allied power in Europe facing the juggernaut of the Nazi war machine. The rapid and comprehensive defeat of the French marked the first major victory for Germany and a massive blow to Allied morale
