On June 22, 1941, Nazi Germany launched Operation Barbarossa, the largest military invasion in history, breaking its non-aggression pact with the Soviet Union. Over 3 million German troops, supported by 3,000 tanks and 2,000 aircraft, stormed across a 2,900-kilometer front. The Nazi forces initially achieved spectacular success, driving deep into Soviet territory and encircling entire Soviet armies. Cities fell one after another as the Wehrmacht advanced toward Moscow, Leningrad, and Stalingrad.
However, the Soviets refused to surrender, implementing a scorched-earth policy and moving their industrial base east of the Ural Mountains. As winter approached, the German advance began to falter against strengthening Soviet resistance and harsh weather conditions. The failure to capture Moscow before winter marked a crucial turning point
